### 1.1 Homepage

**Primary Goal**: Establish brand authority and communicate core value proposition. Must rank for broad terms like "website laten maken" while immediately differentiating KNAP GEMAAKT.

**Psychological Triggers**:
- **Specificity**: €595 and 7 dagen are concrete, memorable, differentiate instantly
- **Risk Reversal**: Satisfaction guarantee removes purchase anxiety (Loss Aversion principle)
- **Authority**: Review counts, years in business, project numbers
- **Cognitive Ease**: "Geen gedoe" reduces anticipated complexity

**Title Formula** (50-60 characters):
```
Website Laten Maken €595 | 7 Dagen | KNAP GEMAAKT.
```

**Alternative Titles**:
- `Professionele Website €595 | Binnen 7 Dagen | KNAP GEMAAKT.`
- `Website Laten Maken? €595 & Binnen 1 Week | KNAP GEMAAKT.`
- `KNAP GEMAAKT. | Professioneel Webdesign Zonder Gedoe v.a. €595`

**Description Formula** (140-155 characters):
```
Professionele website voor €595 vast, binnen 7 dagen live. Geen gedoe, inclusief tevredenheidsgarantie. Vraag vandaag je gratis offerte aan.
```

**Alternative Descriptions**:
- `Zoek je een professionele website? Bij KNAP GEMAAKT. krijg je een complete website voor €595. Klaar in 7 dagen. Plan je gratis gesprek!`
- `Website laten maken? ✓ €595 vast ✓ 7 dagen levertijd ✓ Geen verborgen kosten ✓ Tevredenheidsgarantie. Start vandaag nog!`

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**:
- Generic "Home – Brand Name" titles (wastes keyword space)
- Listing all services instead of leading with USP
- Missing price and speed differentiators
- No call-to-action in description
- Using "Goedkoop" instead of specific price (cheap implies low quality)
- Placing brand name first when not yet a household name

## Part 2: Keyword Strategy in Meta Tags

### 2.1 Keyword Placement in Titles

**Front-Loading Principle**: Place primary keyword within first 2-3 words.

**Why**:
- Eye-tracking shows F-shaped reading pattern (users scan beginnings)
- Mobile screens truncate after ~50-60 characters
- If keyword is at end, may be cut off

**Examples**:
- ✅ `Webdesign Utrecht | €595 | KNAP GEMAAKT.`
- ❌ `KNAP GEMAAKT. | Uw Partner voor Webdesign Utrecht`

**Exact Match vs. Variation**:
- **Title**: Use exact match for primary keyword ("Webshop laten maken")
- **Description**: Use natural variations ("Online winkel starten", "E-commerce oplossing")
- Google's BERT/MUM understand context - "Website laten maken" and "Laat je website maken" are same intent

### 2.2 Keyword Placement in Descriptions

**Frequency**: Include primary keyword once or twice naturally, preferably in first sentence.

**Bolding Effect**: Google bolds terms matching search query - this draws eyes to your listing.

**Strategy**:
- If targeting "Website laten maken", ensure exact words appear early
- Triggers bolding = visual beacon in sea of results

**Formula**: `[Keyword Match] + [Unique Value] + [Call to Action]`

**Balance**: Prioritize persuasion over keyword density. Keyword-packed descriptions that lack value proposition may rank but won't convert.

### 2.3 Secondary Keywords & Semantic SEO

**LSI Keywords are a myth** - Google confirmed they don't use Latent Semantic Indexing.

**Instead, use Semantic SEO**: Cover topics comprehensively with naturally related terms.

**For "Webdesign" page, include related entities**:
- WordPress, responsive, SEO-vriendelijk, huisstijl, onderhoud, mobiel-vriendelijk

**Long-Tail in Descriptions**: Use description to catch modifiers too verbose for title.
- Title: "Website laten maken"
- Description adds: "...geschikt voor ZZP en MKB"

### 2.4 Local Keywords

**Service Area Business (SAB) Strategy**: KNAP GEMAAKT. serves multiple cities without physical office in each.

**Structure**: `[Service] + [City]`

**Placement**:
- **Title**: Must contain [City] to rank in Local Pack
- **Description**: Reinforce connection ("Wij zijn actief in [City] en omstreken")

**Handling Multiple Cities**:
- Create unique pages for top 10-15 target cities
- Group smaller towns into region pages ("Webdesign Rivierenland")
- Never list 50 cities in one description (spam signal)

### 2.5 Brand Name in Titles

**For KNAP GEMAAKT. (not yet household name)**:
- Brand name goes at **END** of title
- Service keyword is what user searches; brand is secondary
- Format: `[Primary Keyword] | [Differentiator] | KNAP GEMAAKT.`

**Exception**: Homepage can feature brand more prominently.

**Never Omit Brand Entirely**: Consistent inclusion builds recognition via Mere Exposure Effect.

**Intent Assignment**:

- Service Pages: Transactional Intent
- Location Pages: Local Transactional Intent
- Blog: Informational Intent

### 3.2 Keyword Placement Hierarchy

| Priority | Element | Guidance |
|----------|---------|----------|
| 1 | **H1 Tag** | One per page, include primary keyword |
| 2 | **Title Tag** | Can differ from H1; optimize for CTR |
| 3 | **First 100 Words** | Include primary keyword early |
| 4 | **H2 Headings** | Include secondary/related keywords |
| 5 | **URL Slug** | Short, descriptive, include keyword |
| 6 | **Meta Description** | Include keyword for bolding |
| 7 | **Image Alt Text** | Descriptive with relevant keywords |
| 8 | **Body Content** | Natural usage, 0.5-2% density |

**Keyword Density 2026**: Target 0.5-2%. For 600-word page = 3-6 natural mentions. Focus on comprehensive topic coverage, not counting.

### 3.3 Internal Linking & Anchor Text

**Anchor Text Best Practices**:
- Use descriptive, varied anchor text
- Avoid generic "click here" or "read more"
- Ratio: 50-60% partial-match, 35-45% branded/generic, max 10% exact-match

**Link Quantity**:
- Service pages: 10-20 contextual internal links
- Blog posts: 5-15 internal links to service/location pages
- All key pages within 3 clicks of homepage

### 3.5 Preventing Cannibalization

**Identification**:
1. Google Search Console → Performance → Filter by query → View Pages
2. Site search: `site:knapgemaakt.nl "website laten maken"`
3. Multiple URLs ranking for same keyword + intent = cannibalization

**Solutions**:

| Scenario | Solution |
|----------|----------|
| Service competes with location page | Differentiate intent: Service = national, Location = local |
| Multiple blogs on similar topic | Consolidate weaker into stronger, 301 redirect |
| Old content competing with new | Update and redirect, or noindex old |

## Part 5: CTR Optimization

### 5.1 Power Words by Intent

**Transactional (Service/Location)**:
- Action: Krijg, Bestel, Start, Vraag aan, Nu, Direct
- Value: Gratis, €595, Inclusief, Geen verborgen kosten, Vast tarief
- Speed: Direct, Vandaag, 7 Dagen, Snel, Binnen 1 week
- Trust: Garantie, Bewezen, Betrouwbaar, Gegarandeerd, Resultaat

**Informational (Blog)**:
- Curiosity: Ontdek, Geheim, Verrassend, Weinig bekend
- Guide: Compleet, Ultiem, Stap-voor-stap, Alles over, Gids
- Warning: Vermijd, Fout, Pas op, Dure fouten, Valkuilen
- Utility: Tips, Checklist, Besparen, Vergelijking, Gratis

**Navigational (About/Contact)**:
- Trust: Ervaren, Professioneel, Betrouwbaar, Eerlijk, Transparant
- Access: Persoonlijk, Vrijblijvend, Direct contact, Toegankelijk

### 5.2 Numbers & Specificity

**The €595 Hook**: Putting price in title is high-risk, high-reward. In low-end market, massive CTR booster - removes "call for quote" friction, qualifies leads instantly.

**Odd Numbers**: "7 Dagen" sticks better than "1 week". "5 Tips" better than "Tips". Odd numbers capture attention more effectively.

**Year Modifier**: Adding "2026" signals current relevance. Only for time-sensitive content. Don't use if you won't update annually.

### 5.3 Symbols & Formatting

**Separators**:
| Symbol | Best Use | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Pipe (\|) | Separating sections | Uses fewer pixels, but Google removes 41% |
| Dash (–) | Continuation | Less likely rewritten by Google |
| Colon (:) | Lists, explanations | Saves space |

**Recommendation**: Use dashes for safety from rewrites; pipes for visual separation.

**Special Characters**:
- ✓ works well: "✓ 7 Dagen ✓ €595 ✓ Garantie"
- € for price always
- → for CTAs
- Avoid: ALL CAPS (spammy), excessive emojis (unprofessional B2B)

### 5.4 Testing & Benchmarks

**A/B Testing Process**:
1. Identify pages with high impressions, low CTR (GSC)
2. Change ONE element (title OR description)
3. Wait 30+ days for data
4. Compare CTR in GSC Performance

**CTR Benchmarks**:
| Position | Target CTR |
|----------|-----------|
| Position 1 | 25%+ |
| Position 2-3 | 10-20% |
| Position 4-10 | 2-10% |
| Branded terms | 20%+ |

**Diagnosis**:
- High rank + low CTR = Title/Description problem
- Low rank = Content/Authority problem

## Part 6: Character Limits & Technical Rules

### 6.1 Character Limits

| Element | Desktop | Mobile | Target |
|---------|---------|--------|--------|
| Meta Title | ~60 chars | ~55 chars | **50-60 characters** |
| Meta Description | ~160 chars | ~120 chars | **140-155 characters** |

### 6.2 The 10 Commandments

1. **Every page gets unique title and description** - no duplicates
2. **Primary keyword in both title and description**
3. **Write for humans first, search engines second**
4. **Include CTA in every description**
5. **Front-load most important information**
6. **Use differentiators** (€595, 7 dagen, geen gedoe)
7. **Match search intent** - informational for blogs, transactional for services
8. **Test and iterate** - review CTR quarterly minimum
9. **Check mobile display** - preview on phone screens
10. **Keep descriptions even if Google rewrites** - well-written more likely kept

## Part 8: Red Flags & Mistakes to Avoid

### 8.1 Title Mistakes

❌ **Keyword stuffing**: "Website Laten Maken | Website Bouwen | Website Maken"
❌ **Too long**: Important info cut off in SERPs
❌ **Too short**: Under 20 chars = 96.6% rewrite rate
❌ **Brand first**: Wastes keyword space for non-household brands
❌ **Duplicates**: Confuses engines, splits authority
❌ **Misleading**: High bounce damages rankings
❌ **Outdated year**: "Website Maken 2023" in 2026 signals neglect
❌ **"Welkom bij..."**: Wasted space, no information

### 8.2 Description Mistakes

❌ **Missing entirely**: Google generates (often poorly)
❌ **Over 160 chars**: Truncation loses CTA
❌ **No CTA**: Missed click opportunity
❌ **Generic**: "Welkom bij onze website" = invisible
❌ **Copied first paragraph**: Lazy, rarely optimized
❌ **Same template all pages**: Signals low quality
❌ **Overusing emojis**: Unprofessional B2B

### 8.3 Keyword Mistakes

❌ **Same keyword multiple pages**: Cannibalization
❌ **No keyword mapping**: Leads to conflicts
❌ **Ignoring intent**: Blog content on transactional page
❌ **Over-optimizing density**: Reads unnaturally
❌ **Template location pages**: Google detects duplicates

### 8.4 Dutch-Specific Mistakes

❌ **Ignoring .nl domain**: Weakens local signals
❌ **Not listing on Dutch directories**: Telefoonboek.nl, Bedrijvengids matter
❌ **Inconsistent NAP**: Address/phone must match GBP exactly
❌ **English keywords for Dutch audience**: "web design" vs "webdesign"
❌ **Missing LocalBusiness schema**: JSON-LD essential

## Part 10: The "Geen Gedoe" Advantage

The phrase "Geen gedoe" is not just a slogan - it's a powerful SEO asset.

**Why It Works**:
- "Gedoe" (hassle/fuss) is significant barrier for small business owners fearing technical complexity
- By optimizing with terms like "Eenvoudig", "Alles geregeld", "Zonder technische kennis"
- KNAP GEMAAKT. targets **psychological intent** (fear of complexity) not just **informational intent** (need for website)

**Blue Ocean Strategy**: This separates the agency from technical competitors focusing on specs like "HTML/CSS" or "Custom API" - positioning in uncontested market space.
